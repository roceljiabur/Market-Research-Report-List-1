<p><p>The insoluble dietary fibers market comprises various types including cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin, and others. Cellulose, derived from plant cell walls, provides structural support and aids digestion. Hemicellulose, a polysaccharide, is found in plant fibers and contributes to digestive health. Lignin, a complex polymer, enhances gut health and offers antioxidant properties. Other types may include resistant starch and various plant fibers. Together, these fibers play a vital role in promoting digestive wellness, reducing chronic disease risk, and enhancing overall health.</p></p>

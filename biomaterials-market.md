<p><ul><li>Metallic Biomaterial</li><li>Ceramic Biomaterial</li><li>Polymeric Biomaterial</li><li>Natural Biomaterial</li><li>Composites</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biomaterials market encompasses several types, each playing a vital role in medical applications. Metallic biomaterials, such as titanium and stainless steel, are known for their strength and durability, often used in implants. Ceramic biomaterials, including hydroxyapatite, offer superior biocompatibility and are used in bone repair. Polymeric biomaterials are versatile, providing flexibility and scalability for devices like stents. Natural biomaterials, sourced from biological organisms, exhibit excellent compatibility. Composites combine different materials to enhance mechanical properties and functionality, catering to diverse medical needs.</p></p>

<p><p>The radon testing market comprises two primary types: radon detectors and radon test kits. Radon detectors are electronic devices that continuously monitor radon levels in real-time, often used in residential and commercial settings for ongoing surveillance. In contrast, radon test kits are simple, typically passive devices that require users to collect samples over a specific period, sending them to a lab for analysis. Both types play a crucial role in assessing indoor air quality and ensuring safety from radon exposure.</p></p>

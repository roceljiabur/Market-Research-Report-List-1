<p><p>The latex polymer market is primarily divided into two types: natural latex polymers and synthetic latex polymers. Natural latex polymers are derived from the sap of rubber trees, offering biodegradability and superior elasticity, making them ideal for applications like gloves and mattresses. In contrast, synthetic latex polymers are man-made, usually from petroleum-based monomers, providing enhanced durability and flexibility. They are widely used in coatings, adhesives, and construction materials, catering to diverse industrial needs and preferences for performance and environmental impact.</p></p>
